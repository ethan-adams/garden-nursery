# Hush Arbor Writing Sample Pack

This pack anchors the first vertical slice tone: warm, specific, hopeful, and willing to let people be complicated.

## Recurring Characters

**Mara Lye, cafe cook**

Mara runs the narrow cafe kitchen behind Bellwether Hall. Her apron is always damp at the hem, her hands smell like parsley stems, and she can stretch one pot of soup farther than anyone should have to. She wants reliable culinary herbs because the cafe depends on thrift disguised as taste. She resents waste, but she still buys one impractical plant each spring and calls it "research."

**Tovan Ree, school garden teacher**

Tovan carries broken pencils, seed labels, and a budget sheet folded into quarters. He believes children deserve wonder and knows wonder still needs someone to haul compost. He asks for sturdy plants with stories, then panics when a tray looks fragile because he has promised too many children that care works if you keep trying.

**Cilla Park, porch gardener**

Cilla recently bought a narrow house with chimney shade, clay soil, and neighbors who notice everything. She jokes before admitting fear. She wants low-effort plants, but not plants that announce she needed help. The right recommendation gives her privacy as much as beauty.

## Customer Barks

- Mara: "If the chives can survive my dishwasher's singing, I'll take two."
- Tovan: "Do you have anything sturdy enough for eight-year-olds with watering cans and legal authority?"
- Cilla: "The porch is mostly shade, but I would like it to look like a choice."
- Mara: "The supplier said tomatoes. The weather board said hubris. Which one do you trust?"
- Tovan: "Pollinators, yes, but nothing that sounds like a moral lesson when I explain it."
- Cilla: "I need low effort, not visibly low effort. There is a difference."
- Mara: "Parsley is not glamorous, but neither is rent."
- Tovan: "The children named the empty bed Captain Mud. We should intervene."
- Cilla: "If that violet droops around arguments, I may need a bulk discount."
- Mara: "Give me herbs that forgive a late bus and an early lunch rush."

## End-Of-Week Reflections

- Mist held in the orchard rows until noon. The customers who bought hardy starts left smug; the tomato people left with instructions and suspicion.
- By closing, the porch gardeners had traded shade measurements like family secrets. Reputation rose in quiet increments.
- The school order did not pay much, but three children pressed their noses to the calendula tray and promised to come back with names.

## Seasonal Event

**First Seed Swap At Bellwether Hall**

Locals bring jars with handwritten labels, half the town pretends not to judge the handwriting, and every generous trade carries a memory. Demand rises for story-rich, hardy, and porch-friendly plants, but bragging about rare stock costs trust. The player can read this as a market chance and a social warning: selling the right starts helps, but treating the swap like a clearance rack does not.
